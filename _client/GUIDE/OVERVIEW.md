# Jig — Merchant Overview

Welcome to Jig. You are using a modern e-commerce engine designed for **editorial confidence**. This guide explains the core concepts of your store and how to use it effectively.

## What is a "Shop" in Jig?

A Shop is your digital showroom. Unlike generic e-commerce platforms that focus on bulk volume, Jig focuses on **presentation**. 

- **Warm Minimalism:** Your products are given "room to breathe" with generous whitespace.
- **Craft-First:** The design is built to highlight the texture, details, and story of your items.
- **Editorial Feel:** Large, bold typography (Syne) makes your brand feel like a high-end magazine.

## Core Components

### 1. The Collection
Your products are organized into **Categories**. 
- **Tip:** Use 5–7 main categories to keep the top navigation strip clean.
- **Imagery:** Every category should have a high-quality cover image.

### 2. The Product Grid
Jig uses a 4-column desktop grid. 
- **Featured Items:** You can mark specific products as "Featured" to display them prominently on the homepage.
- **Variants:** You can add different sizes, colours, or materials for each product, each with its own stock level.

### 3. The Bag (Cart)
We use a slide-out drawer instead of a separate cart page. This keeps the customer on their shopping journey without interruptions.

## How Sellers Use Jig

1. **Inventory Management:** Use the Admin Panel (`/admin`) to add products, set prices, and manage stock.
2. **Order Tracking:** When a customer buys something via Paystack, the order appears in your dashboard. You can update the status from "Paid" to "Fulfilled".
3. **Content Control:** You can edit the homepage text (Hero headings and subtext) directly from the "Pages" collection in the admin panel.
